Development Update #5:
Smart Contract Flow

Recap: March 22, 2018

This is the fifth in a series of developmental updates for Matcheos. We have now browsed through profiles, submitted offers, received matches, and entered into a smart contract. In this post, we explore the mechanics of a Matcheos Smart Contract. The focus is on how two people who've just met, can use the incentives of a staked contract to enhance the odds of a successful relationship.

Smart Contract Acceptance:

Acceptance

After you've agreed to the Matcheos Terms and Code of Conduct, a smart contract is created on the EOS blockchain. This contract is unique to each match.

Staking EOS

Once the contract has been deployed on the blockchain, you then send your EOS offer (see update #1) from your wallet to the smart contract account.

Interaction Flow

A Matcheos contract is just a back and forth communication between 2 people that proceeds in steps. It's sort of like a chat towards a goal.

Releasing the EOS:

Step Release

At each step in the contract, one person takes a turn at communication: either responding or asking questions. After the step, some EOS is released back to each person. So once the goal is reached, the EOS should be returned.

Total Steps

The total number of steps is built into the contract. Each step is one person having a turn in the interaction.

Incentives

Because people have EOS at stake, they are incentivized to behave. Acting like a troll may cause EOS forfeiture.

Ending a Contract:

Goal Achieved

A contract proceeds in a back and forth manner. By the end of the contract, you must decide whether to agree to the goal or terminate the contract early.

Early Termination

You don't have to reach the goal. Either person can terminate the contract at any step. If you terminate, there is a 24 hour EOS release delay to prevent arbitration frontrunning.

New Contracts

If you finish a contract and achieve the goal, you have the option of entering into a new contract with the same person.

Arbitration:

Code of Conduct

At any time during the contract, either person can request arbitration over a breach in the Code of Conduct. A person must provide clear evidence of a breach in the Code.

Disputes

Disputes are resolved in binding arbitration. All decisions are final. Any forfeited EOS is donated to a charity set forth in the terms.

Fee

The person who requests arbitration must pay a small fee. This fee is determined in the terms of the contract.

Reviews:

More Incentives

Matcheos includes a number of incentives to enforce good behavior between people. One such system is a review system for each contract.

During Contract

Each person can review the other person's behavior during the contract. These reviews are displayed to other Matcheos users at the time of match acceptance.

After Contract

Similarly, you can provide a review for how the person behaves offline - assuming there is additional interaction after the contract ends.

More Details:

That's it for this post. This finishes up the basic skeleton of a minimum system. Obviously, there are many more features that can be added. But, for now, the feature set has been locked until the Alpha version has been completed. Stay tuned.
