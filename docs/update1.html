Development Update #1:
Profiles and Bidding

Introduction: March 4, 2018

This is the first in a series of developmental updates for Matcheos. This post will focus on the user interface for browsing through Matcheos profiles and bidding. The next post (see here) will focus on the matching algorithm. Expect this update in a few days.


Creating a profile:

When you join Matcheos, you create a profile that includes the following: 1) Your email; 2) A picture; 3) First name; 4) Your location; 5) A tagline (e.g. job @ company or student @ university). Importantly, this information needs to be accurate because Matcheos will have a Code of Conduct. If you breach the Code of Conduct (for example, by creating sock puppets or fake pictures), the EOS you stake in the smart contract are subject to being forfeited.

A Sample Profile:

Bidding

You browse through profiles and submit bids. Bids are matched using an algorithm. Accepted matches are handled through a smart contract.

Date Bid

This is the equivalent of an online dating feature (like Tinder, etc.). You bid if you want to date that person.

Friend Bid

This is equivalent to friending or following on something like Facebook or Twitter. You bid if you want to befriend.

Work Bid

This is equivalent to a networking connection on something like LinkedIn. You bid to connect.

Bidding:

Bidding

As you browse through each profile you can either skip, date bid, work bid, or friend bid.

Non-Binding

Bids are non-binding offers. They don't actually require you to transfer EOS from your account.

Limits

You won't be able to bid more than your EOS account balance. So you need EOS to use Matcheos.

Multiple Bids

You can bid on a person more than once for different purposes (date vs. friend).

Another Sample Profile:

Bidding

The person you bid on does not know that you bid on them. A bid is only revealed when a match is made.

Date Bid

This is the equivalent of an online dating feature (like Tinder, etc.). You bid if you want to date that person.

Friend Bid

This is equivalent to friending or following on something Facebook or Twitter. You bid if you want to befriend.

Work Bid

This is equivalent to a networking connection on something like LinkedIn. You bid to connect.

More Details:

A profile contains a minimum amount of information to make signup as easy as possible. It's noteworthy to understand that if you enter into a match, the first phases of the smart contract will contain steps to transfer additional information. So things like social media profiles or resumes are steps in the smart contract after a match has been made.
