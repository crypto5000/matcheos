Arbitration.

By agreeing to enter into a Matcheos Smart Contract (the "Contract") you are subject to binding arbitration per the Contract terms.

Arbitation Decisions.

All arbitration decisions are final and at the discretion of the arbitrator. The arbitrator shall be designated at the beginning of the Contract, including any arbitration fees that must be paid during arbitration. Any penalties or forfeits may be donated to a charity per the Contract terms.


Code Provisions.

Subject to the Contract, either party may ask for arbitration if one of the following provisions has been breached. The party asking for arbitration must provide evidence of said breach.

Provision 1: No threats may be made to the other party. This includes, but is not limited to, threats of violence toward a person or a person's relationships, threats of emotional abuse, or threats of finanical ruin.

Provision 2: No scams may be initiated towards the other party. This includes, but is not limited to, attempting to run ponzi schemes, investment chirades, or fake companies.

Provision 3: No trolling may be initiated towards the other party. This includes, but is not limited to, repeated harassment, discrimatory language, or sexist or racist comments.

Provision 4: No hacks may be initiated towards the other party. This includes, but is not limited to, attempting to procure private keys, passwords, account information, or other financial information.

Provision 5: No theft may be initiated towards the other party. This includes, but is not limited to, stealing documents, identity information, or employment secrets.

Provision 6: No bribes may be initiated towards the other party. This includes, but is not limited to, paying for votes or paying for reputation metrics.

Provision 7: No doxxing may be initiated towards the other party. This includes, but is not limited to, exposing a person relationships against their will, or exposing a person's information against their will.

Provision 8: No soliciting may be initiated towards the other party. This includes, but is not limited to, soliciting a person to join an organization against their will or soliciting a donation against their will.

Provision 9: No commerce may be initiated towards the other party. This includes, but is not limited to, trying to sell goods and services on the Matcheos platform.

Provision 10: No sexually explicit material may be posted to the other party. This includes, but is not limited to, nude photos or other suggestive language or text.

Provision 11: No human trafficking may be initiated towards the other party. This includes, but is not limited to, trying to entice the other person into sex trafficking.

Provision 12: No sex work may be initiated towards the other party. This includes, but is not limited to, prostitution or other sexual activity.

Provision 13: No begging may be initiated towards the other party. This includes, but is not limited to, asking for tips or donations.

Provision 14: No illegal activity may be initiated towards the other party, subject to the legal jurisdication of the people in the Contract.

Arbitration Discretion.

It is at the discretion of the arbitrator to determine if a penalty should be enforced and what the penalty should be. Penalties include forfeited staked tokens and account closure.

The arbitrator has the discretion to penalize the party requesting arbitration, if it is deemed that this person breached the Code of Conduct.

Modification of the Code.

Matcheos reserves the right, at its sole discretion, to modify or replace any of these provisions, or change, suspend, or discontinue the Service (including without limitation, the availability of any feature, database, or content) at any time by posting a notice on the Site or by sending you notice through the Service or via email. Matcheos may also impose limits on certain features and services or restrict your access to parts or all of the Service without notice or liability. It is your responsibility to check this Code periodically for changes. Your continued use of the Service following the posting of any changes to the Code constitutes acceptance of those changes.
