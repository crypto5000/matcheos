A New Way to Meet People
using the EOS platform.

A Bidding Process + Smart Contracts = EOS Match.
Bids optimize for preferences and smart contracts enforce good behavior.

Matcheos Use Cases:

Dating

Browse through profiles and submit bids for people you'd like to date. After a match is found, the smart contract enhances good behavior.

Work

Employees or employers can also browse and submit bids. HR recruiters and potential employees can bid for interview matches.

Friends

You can use Matcheos for casual purposes. For example, groups like fraternities and sororities can use the system to find new members.

The Matcheos Process:

How it Works

You browse through profiles and submit bids. Bids are matched using an algorithm. Accepted matches are handled through a smart contract.

Bidding

Higher bids signal a higher preference to meet. For example, Alice can submit a bid of 10 EOS to meet Bob and 2 EOS to meet Charlie. Bob can likewise submit bids for Alice and Eve.

Matching

An algorithm matches people based on bids. You then have the option to accept. If both people accept, they send their bids to a smart contract.

Meeting

As the meeting occurs, the smart contract releases EOS back to people in discrete stages. This ensures that both parties have an incentive to behave.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Question: What is Matcheos?

Answer: It's a new way to meet people using EOS.

Question: What is the primary use for Matcheos?

Answer: The primary use is probably dating. But who knows?

Question: How far along is Matcheos?

Answer: Matcheos is just starting development.

Question: What's new about Matcheos (#1)?

Answer: You bid on people using EOS. Higher bids increase the odds of a match.

Question: What's new about Matcheos (#2)?

Answer: A match is handled through an EOS smart contract.

Question: How is a match made?

Answer: It's a giant math problem using people's EOS bids.

Question: Do I spend EOS to bid?

Answer: No. Bids are just offers. But if you accept a match, you'll need EOS.

Question: Do I lockup EOS to meet?

Answer: Yes. You need EOS to send an accepted bid into the smart contract.

Question: How many matches will I get?

Answer: This dependes on the bids. You may not get any matches.

Question: What if I don't like the match?

Answer: You don't have to accept a match.

Question: Why do I have to stake EOS in a contract?

Answer: This provides an incentive to behave during the match meeting.

Question: How is the EOS released in a contract?

Answer: The EOS is in a multisig wallet and is released by both parties.

Question: Where can I get support?

Answer: Open an issue on github here.
